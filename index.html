About

I am a Professor of Economics at the Indian Institute of Management Indore, where I have been based since 2015. My research lies at the intersection of labour economics, development economics, urban and spatial economics, competition policy, and industrial organisation.

My work places a strong emphasis on quasi-experimental causal inference, applied to Indian administrative and large-scale survey data — including PLFS, NFHS, NSS, IHDS, CMIE, and SHRUG. I am particularly interested in how infrastructure, institutions, and policy interventions shape labour market outcomes, household behaviour, and market structure in developing economies.

I hold an active ANRF Matrics Grant focused on game-theoretic models for competition policy in Indian markets. I supervise PhD students working on competition policy, green economics, migration, and marriage markets.

Research Interests

My research spans the following fields and themes:

Labour Economics Development Economics Urban & Spatial Economics Competition Policy Industrial Organisation Causal Inference Gender Economics Intra-household Bargaining Migration Rural–Urban Commuting Infrastructure & Development PMGSY Roads Structural Transformation

Methodologically, I specialise in staggered difference-in-differences (Callaway–Sant'Anna, Sun–Abraham), fuzzy and sharp RDD, instrumental variables / 2SLS, event studies, and synthetic control methods.
